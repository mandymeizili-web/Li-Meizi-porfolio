Data Analysis & Visualization

An Unbalanced Scale — Hong Kong's Dietary Risk Profile

Project Background: This project originated from an unexpected discovery. While visualizing the UN FAO’s 2024 dietary data to identify obesity-related risks, the most striking outlier was not the United States, but Hong Kong. My analysis, focusing on meat, sugar, fats, and alcohol, uncovered a critical public health signal.

Key Findings: A comparative bar chart revealed that Hong Kong's per capita energy from meat is a staggering 731 kcal/day, rivaling the US and dwarfing regional averages. The true insight, however, lies in its dietary structure. Unlike the US, where energy intake is spread across fats and sugars, Hong Kong exhibits an overwhelmingly "meat-centric" dietary pattern. This extreme imbalance is a direct visual alarm for public health, as high meat consumption is consistently linked to increased risks of obesity and cardiovascular disease.

Value & Impact: The core value of this project is its ability to distill a complex nutritional issue into a single, powerful insight. Through precise data filtering and intuitive visualization, I transformed a vast dataset into an easily understandable warning: Hong Kong's dietary scale is dangerously tipped. It demonstrates the power of data visualization to make hidden risks "speak for themselves," providing clear, actionable evidence for public health intervention. The immediate recommendation is to prioritize optimizing dietary structure, shifting focus from animal to plant-based sources to mitigate a long-term health crisis.
